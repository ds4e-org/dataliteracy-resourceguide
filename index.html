About Our Coalition

Data Science for Everyone

Data Science for Everyone is a coalition advancing data science education so that every K-12 student is equipped with the data literacy skills needed to succeed in our modern world. Equitable access to data science education is an opportunity to open doors to higher education, high-paying careers, and an engaged community.

Created by the University of Chicago Center for RISC and organized in partnership with The Learning Agency and the Concord Consortium, we support a growing community that knows that the data revolution has transformed modern life and we need to prepare our students.

K-5 Resources for Data Literacy and Data Science

What is Data Literacy?

Data literacy refers to the ability to read, understand, interpret, and communicate data or claims derived from data. In elementary education, data literacy involves teaching students how to iteratively question data and representations of data critically, including understanding their origins, limitations and potential biases.

Sample Learning Progressions

K-2.A.1.2a: Recognize the importance of asking questions about how data were collected.
K-2.A.1.3a: Observe that data can have many different answers or results.
3-5.A.1.2a: Ask questions about how data are collected or considered.
3-5.A.1.2b: Understand that data is generated by people who make decisions about what and how to measure.

6-8 Resources for Data Literacy and Data Science

What is Data Science?

Data science education refers to instruction focused on systematic processes, analytical techniques, and utilization of appropriate technologies to gain knowledge from data. In middle school, it equips students with introductory skills and problem-solving necessary to collect, analyze, interpret, and communicate data to answer investigative questions.

Sample Learning Progressions

6-8.A.1.1a: Analyze the way categorical and numeric data shapes its interpretation and analysis.
6-8.A.1.1b: Recognize that numerical variables may be either discrete or continuous.

9-12 Resources for Data Science

Data Science in High School

Data science education in high school encompasses systematic processes, analytical techniques, and technologies to gain knowledge from data. This includes exploratory data analysis, visualization, modeling, data ethics, machine learning, artificial intelligence, and professional practices of statistics and data science.

Sample Learning Progressions

9-10.A.1.1a: Define "qualitative" and "quantitative" and understand how they relate to categorical and numeric data.
9-10.A.1.1b: Understand that forms of media can be represented in quantitative and qualitative terms.
11-12.A.1.1a: Recognize that multiple types of data can provide valuable insights into the same inquiry.
